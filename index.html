What is Delf?

  • DELF stands for "Diplôme d'Études en Langue Française," which translates to "Diploma in French Studies" in English. It is an official certification awarded by the French Ministry of Education to individuals who successfully demonstrate their proficiency in the French language. DELF exams are designed to assess language skills in four key areas: listening, speaking, reading, and writing. The exams are divided into different levels (A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2), with each level corresponding to a certain degree of proficiency. These certifications are widely recognized internationally and can be beneficial for academic, professional, or personal purposes.
